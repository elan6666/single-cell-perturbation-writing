# Drug-Perturbation Evidence Ladder

Use this sequence before drafting Results and again in review.

1. **Exposure reality:** drug, dose, time, vehicle, context, replicate support,
   and viability/composition context.
2. **Matched predictive fidelity:** no-effect and strong input-matched baseline,
   aggregated by condition or drug.
3. **Drug-specific generalization:** the stated held-out drug, scaffold, MoA,
   dose, time, donor, context, or combination without leakage.
4. **Mechanistic consistency:** target, pathway, signature, or responder-state
   agreement after quantitative evidence.
5. **Decision utility:** prioritization only against a stated assay endpoint.
6. **Failure map:** chemical distance, support, dose, time, effect strength,
   viability, and context define the operating boundary.

Check whether generic stress, apoptosis, cell-cycle, batch, or average-response
signals explain performance before calling it drug-specific. Do not infer
efficacy, safety, binding, PK/PD, or clinical benefit without those endpoints.
